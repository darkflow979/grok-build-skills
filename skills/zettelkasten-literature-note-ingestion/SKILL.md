\### Purpose



Produce a high-quality literature note that captures the source accurately while preparing atomic ideas for linking and synthesis.



\### When to use



\- When adding new research material (Zotero, PDFs, articles, books)

\- When you want to quickly move from raw source to structured note + atomic candidates

\- During essay research phases to maintain consistent note quality



\### When not to use



\- Very short or trivial sources that don’t warrant a full note

\- Highly personal or sensitive material (handle manually)

\- When you need deep critical analysis immediately (this skill focuses on ingestion)



\### Inputs



\- Source material (Zotero export, PDF highlights, web article, text excerpt)

\- Access to Driftworks vault and Zotero integration (if used)

\- Your current research focus or essay topic (optional but helpful)



\### Procedure



1\. Capture basic metadata (title, author, year, source link).

2\. Create or open a literature note using your preferred template.

3\. Extract key ideas, quotes, and your initial reactions.

4\. Suggest 2–5 atomic ideas that could become separate notes.

5\. Add meaningful outgoing links to existing notes where relevant.

6\. Write at least one original sentence connecting the source to your thinking.

7\. Tag and frontmatter as appropriate.

8\. Review for accuracy and active engagement (avoid passive summary).



\### Output



\- Completed literature note in Driftworks

\- 2–5 suggested atomic note ideas (with titles and brief descriptions)

\- Updated links and metadata



\### Failure modes



\- Creating overly long literature notes that are hard to link

\- Passive summarization without original thought

\- Missing important context or misrepresenting the source

\- Forgetting to add outgoing links



\### Examples



\*\*Example 1\*\*: Ingesting a paper on AI infrastructure governance → literature note with key mechanisms + atomic notes on verification layers and ontology drift.



\*\*Example 2\*\*: Processing Zotero highlights from a Luhmann text → literature note + atomic notes on second-order observation and communication.



\### Provenance



Distilled from repeated ingestion sessions while building and using the Driftworks vault.
